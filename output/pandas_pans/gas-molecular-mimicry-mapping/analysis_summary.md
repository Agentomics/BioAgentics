## 1. Executive Summary

A systematic computational screen of the Group A Streptococcus (GAS) proteome (6 serotypes: M1, M3, M5, M12, M18, M49; 10,691 total proteins) against 328 human basal ganglia-enriched proteins identified **8 mimicry targets** passing sequence similarity filters (pident >= 40%, alignment >= 8aa, E-value <= 1e-3). These were scored using a 7-component composite system integrating sequence identity, B-cell epitope overlap, MHC-II cross-reactivity, serotype conservation, FasBCAX phase weighting, query coverage, and known target recovery.

**Top 3 novel mimicry target candidates:**

| Rank | Target | GAS Homolog | Score | Evidence Tier | Key Finding |
|------|--------|------------|-------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | **HSPA6** (Heat shock 70 kDa protein 6) | DnaK (HSP70) | 0.845 | HIGH | Novel for PANDAS; 12 overlapping B-cell epitopes; invasive-phase protein; connected to TUBB3 in autoantibody PPI network |
| 2 | **ENO2** (Neuron-specific enolase) | Streptococcal enolase | 0.784 | HIGH | Semi-known autoantigen (Dale 2006); TM=0.66 structural mimicry; seed protein in autoantibody network (degree 119) |
| 3 | **MCCC1-like** (Methylcrotonoyl-CoA carboxylase alpha) | Biotin-dependent carboxylase | 0.740 | MODERATE | Novel; strong epitope overlap but no literature support |

**Positive control:** GAPDH correctly recovered at rank 4 (score 0.624), validating the pipeline.

## 2. HSPA6/DnaK Mimicry Hypothesis

The top-ranked novel finding is the identification of **HSPA6 (HSP70B') as a potential autoantibody target in PANDAS**, based on molecular mimicry with GAS DnaK.

### Evidence Chain

1. **Sequence mimicry:** GAS DnaK shares 49.3% identity with human HSPA6 over 523 aligned residues (80% query coverage), conserved across all 6 GAS serotypes tested.

2. **B-cell epitope cross-reactivity:** 12 overlapping B-cell epitope pairs identified in the DnaK-HSPA6 aligned region (best cross-reactivity score: 0.606) — the highest epitope overlap of any target.

3. **MHC-II presentation:** Cross-reactive MHC-II binding predicted across all 3 tested HLA-DR alleles (DRB1*04:01, *07:01, *01:01), with up to 196 cross-reactive peptide pairs per allele. Best MHC cross-reactivity score: 6.0 (highest of all targets).

4. **FasBCAX phase:** DnaK classified as invasive-phase protein (2.0x weight), meaning it is preferentially expressed during GAS tissue invasion — the infection phase most relevant to immune exposure.

5. **PPI network integration:** HSPA6 is present in the autoantibody-target-network-mapping PPI network as an interactor, directly connected to **TUBB3** (beta-tubulin-3), a member of the tubulin family that is a known PANDAS autoantibody target.

6. **Patient subgroup relevance:** HSPA6 appears in 2 patient subgroups (broad_autoimmunity, cunningham_classic) in the autoantibody network analysis.

7. **Biological mechanism:** HSP70 family proteins act as danger-associated molecular patterns (DAMPs), activating innate immunity via TLR2/TLR4 → NF-kB. Anti-HSPA6 autoantibodies generated by DnaK mimicry could: (a) disrupt HSPA6 chaperone function in neurons, (b) cross-react with tubulin targets via the HSPA6-TUBB3 interaction, (c) trigger TLR-mediated cytokine amplification.

8. **Precedent:** Anti-HSP70 IgG autoantibodies have been detected in CSF of multiple sclerosis patients (Yokota 2006, PMID: 16337948), demonstrating that anti-HSP70 autoimmunity can occur in neuroinflammatory CNS disease.

### Structural Note

HSPA6 has low structural TM-scores with DnaK (0.08-0.17), indicating mimicry operates at the **epitope/peptide level rather than whole-fold structural level**. This is consistent with the hypothesis that specific shared epitope regions drive cross-reactive antibody binding, not global structural similarity.

## 3. Scoring Validation

### Methodology
- 7-component weighted composite: epitope (0.20), MHC (0.20), conservation (0.15), identity (0.15), coverage (0.10), phase (0.10), known_target (0.10)
- Min-max normalization per component; custom phase mapping

### Sensitivity Analysis
- **Top-3 ranking stable** across all 14 single-weight perturbations of +-0.05
- **Top-3 stable** for 13/14 perturbations of +-0.10
- **Stable under equal weights** (1/7 each) and immunology-heavy weights (epitope=0.30, mhc=0.30)
- Only instability: removing phase weight entirely (0.10→0.00) causes ENO2 to narrowly overtake HSPA6

### GAPDH Positive Control
- Rank 4, score 0.624 — biologically plausible
- Zero B-cell epitope overlap but strong MHC-II signal (0.842 normalized)
- Suggests GAPDH mimicry may operate primarily through T-cell mediated mechanisms
- Known_target bonus (0.10 weight) appropriately rewards recovery of established targets

### Differential mimicry:
- No targets found exclusively in PANDAS-associated serotypes (M1, M3, M5, M12, M18) vs non-PANDAS M49
- The mimicry landscape is largely shared, supporting the view that GAS pathogenicity differences arise from factors other than molecular mimicry alone (e.g., superantigen expression, immune evasion, colonization factors)

## 7. Limitations

1. **Small target set (n=8):** Only 8 human targets passed DIAMOND filters. The true mimicry landscape may be larger — relaxing thresholds (e.g., pident >= 30%) would increase hits but also false positives.
2. **Computational predictions only:** B-cell epitope predictions use classical scales (~60-70% accuracy). MHC-II predictions use PSSM-based approximations. No experimental validation.
3. **3 HLA alleles:** MHC-II analysis limited to DRB1*04:01, *07:01, *01:01. PANDAS susceptibility may involve additional alleles (DQ, DP).
4. **No non-brain control:** The screen targets basal ganglia-enriched proteins but doesn't compare against a non-brain control proteome to confirm brain specificity.
5. **Structural analysis:** Limited to AlphaFold predictions; experimental structures would be more reliable. TM-score calculation used simplified CA-only alignment.
6. **Pre-selection bias:** All results are conditional on the DIAMOND screen (pident >= 40%, E-value <= 1e-3). This filter is appropriate for epitope-level mimicry but may miss lower-identity structural mimicry.

## 8. Recommended Follow-Up Experiments

1. **Experimental validation of HSPA6 as autoantibody target:** Test PANDAS patient sera for anti-HSPA6 IgG reactivity using ELISA or Western blot with recombinant HSPA6.
2. **DnaK-HSPA6 cross-reactivity assay:** Immunize mice with recombinant GAS DnaK and test for anti-HSPA6 cross-reactive antibodies.
3. **Epitope mapping:** Synthesize predicted shared epitope peptides (DnaK/HSPA6 aligned region) and test for IgG binding in PANDAS sera using peptide arrays.
4. **TLR signaling assay:** Test whether anti-HSPA6 antibodies can modulate TLR2/TLR4-mediated signaling in microglial cells.
5. **Broader serotype analysis:** Extend screen to emm types beyond the 6 tested, particularly emm49.8 (emerging dominant in England 2025-26).
6. **ENO2 validation:** Confirm enolase cross-reactivity in a PANDAS-specific cohort (prior work was in Sydenham chorea).
