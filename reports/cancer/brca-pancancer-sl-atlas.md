## Executive Summary

This atlas systematically maps gene dependencies and drug sensitivities in BRCA1/2-deficient cell lines across all cancer types in DepMap 25Q3, aiming to identify non-homologous recombination (non-HR) therapeutic vulnerabilities beyond PARP inhibition and discover combination strategies for PARPi-resistant patients.

From 2,119 DepMap cell lines, 99 were classified as BRCA-deficient (34 BRCA1, 68 BRCA2, 3 both), with 137 VUS-carrying lines excluded. Four cancer types qualified for analysis: Bowel and Ovary/Fallopian Tube (primary, >=10 deficient lines with CRISPR data) and Breast and Uterus (exploratory). Among BRCA-deficient lines, 18 (18.2%) showed 53BP1/SHLD complex loss — a known PARPi resistance marker.

**Key findings:**

1. **Established HR pathway positive controls did not reach FDR significance.** None of the 8 positive control genes (PARP1, RAD51C, RAD51D, XRCC2, XRCC3, POLQ, RPA1, POLB) reached FDR < 0.05 in any cancer type, reflecting statistical power limitations with 6-13 BRCA-deficient lines per lineage. However, effect sizes were in the expected direction for PARP1 (best d = -1.12), XRCC2 (d = -1.21), and POLQ (d = -1.03).

2. **53BP1/SHLD negative control behaved as expected.** SHLD-lost BRCA-deficient lines showed reduced PARP1 dependency (pan-cancer d = 0.21), confirming that 53BP1/SHLD loss restores HR and diminishes PARP1 synthetic lethality. This validates the annotation layer.

3. **Genome-wide screen identified 3,709 novel non-HR dependency genes.** Using nominal thresholds (p < 0.05, |d| > 0.3), 5,760 SL hits were found across 4 cancer types. FANCE (Fanconi anemia pathway) recurred in 3 cancer types, with BRCA1-specific hits enriched for FA pathway genes (FANCF, FANCL, FANCM). BRCA1 and BRCA2 showed largely distinct SL profiles (1,252 BRCA1-specific, 1,687 BRCA2-specific, 200 shared), consistent with their different biological roles.

4. **STING1 and TBK1 show suggestive BRCA2-specific dependency in bowel cancer.** STING1 (d = -0.99, FDR = 0.31) and TBK1 (d = -1.09, FDR = 0.45) showed large effect sizes in BRCA2-deficient bowel cancer lines, suggesting cGAS-STING pathway engagement that warrants further investigation.

5. **PRISM drug analysis was limited by PARPi absence.** No clinical PARPi compounds (olaparib, talazoparib, niraparib, rucaparib) were available in PRISM 24Q2. The only named DDR agent (ATR inhibitor 2) showed a modest pan-cancer signal (d = 0.12). A genome-wide drug screen identified 196 nominally BRCA-selective compounds (0 FDR-significant) and 20 combination candidates.

6. **An estimated 45,223 US patients per year carry BRCA1/2 mutations across 15 cancer types.** Nine cancer types representing ~13,505 patients/year lack approved PARPi therapies, representing significant unmet clinical need. Breast cancer (15,536), prostate (10,090), and pancreatic (3,322) carry the largest patient populations.

## Important Caveats

- **Small sample sizes are the dominant limitation.** BRCA-deficient cell lines numbered only 6-18 per cancer type with CRISPR data, severely constraining statistical power. No positive controls reached FDR significance, and the genome-wide screen relied on nominal p-values (not FDR-corrected). All results should be considered hypothesis-generating.
- **The project auto-advanced through analysis and validation stages** without dedicated analyst or validation scientist review. Results were generated by the developer pipeline and have not undergone independent scientific audit.
- **PRISM drug validation was severely constrained.** None of the 4 clinically relevant PARPi compounds were in the PRISM 24Q2 library. Drug sensitivity results are exploratory only.
- **BRCA classification is binary and incomplete.** Lines were classified by LOF mutations and homozygous deletions only. Epigenetic silencing (BRCA1 promoter methylation), heterozygous deletions with LOH, and somatic reversions were not captured.
- **Cell line models may not recapitulate tumor microenvironment effects.** The cGAS-STING pathway findings (STING1, TBK1) are based on cell-autonomous proliferation screens and may underestimate immune-mediated dependencies relevant in vivo.

## Background

BRCA1 and BRCA2 are the most clinically actionable tumor suppressors in oncology. They encode proteins essential for homologous recombination (HR) DNA repair: BRCA1 controls DNA end resection and damage signaling, while BRCA2 loads RAD51 recombinase onto single-stranded DNA. Loss of either protein renders cells dependent on error-prone repair pathways — a vulnerability exploited by PARP inhibitors (PARPi).

Six PARPi are FDA-approved (olaparib, niraparib, rucaparib, talazoparib, veliparib, fuzuloparib) across breast, ovarian, prostate, and pancreatic cancers. However, 30-50% of BRCA-mutant patients either fail to respond or develop acquired resistance. Known resistance mechanisms include BRCA reversion mutations (~60% of PARPi-resistant breast cancers), 53BP1/SHLD complex loss (restoring HR), RAD51C promoter demethylation, drug efflux pump upregulation, and pre-replication complex loss (PNAS 2025). Therapeutic options after PARPi failure remain limited.

BRCA1/2 mutations affect an estimated 80,000+ new US cancer patients annually across breast (~5%), high-grade serous ovarian (~15%), prostate (~3.5%), and pancreatic (~5%) cancers. Most synthetic lethality research focuses on the HR pathway itself (RAD51, ATR, CHK1, WEE1). This atlas takes a systematic, genome-wide approach to discover non-HR dependencies that may be overlooked by candidate-gene studies — metabolic rewiring, epigenetic vulnerabilities, DNA repair pathway crosstalk, and immune signaling dependencies.

Emerging clinical data support the value of this approach: POLtheta inhibitors (ART6043, Artios Pharma) received FDA Fast Track designation in February 2026 for BRCA-mutant breast cancer, and novobiocin (a POLtheta ATPase inhibitor) is in Phase 1 for DDR-deficient solid tumors. CRISPR screens have identified POLB as a PARPi-sensitizer exclusively in BRCA-mutant cells (Tango Therapeutics, PMID 40326293). These candidates motivated inclusion of specific priority targets in this atlas.

## Methodology

### Data Sources

- **DepMap 25Q3:** CRISPR gene effect scores (Chronos), mutation calls (damaging/hotspot/other), copy number (gene-level), for 2,119 cell lines
- **PRISM 24Q2:** Drug sensitivity (log-fold-change viability) across ~500+ compounds
- **TCGA PanCanAtlas:** Somatic mutation frequencies across 15 cancer types (~6,700+ samples)
- **SEER:** US annual cancer incidence estimates for patient population calculations

### Phase 1: BRCA1/2 Classification and Annotation

Cell lines were classified by BRCA1 and BRCA2 status independently:
- **Deficient:** LOF (damaging) mutation AND/OR homozygous deletion
- **Proficient:** No damaging mutations, no homozygous deletion
- **Excluded:** VUS (non-damaging missense) only

Co-annotations applied to all BRCA-deficient lines:
- 53BP1/SHLD complex status (TP53BP1, SHLD1, SHLD2, SHLD3, RIF1, REV7) — any LOF = "SHLD-lost"
- Pre-replication complex status (MCM2-7, ORC1-6, CDC6, CDT1) — any LOF = "pre-RC compromised"
- TP53, PTEN co-mutation status

Cancer types qualified at two tiers:
- **Primary:** >=10 BRCA-deficient lines with CRISPR data
- **Exploratory:** >=5 BRCA-deficient lines (any data)

### Phase 2: HR Pathway Dependency Validation

For each qualifying cancer type and BRCA group (any, BRCA1-only, BRCA2-only):
- Mann-Whitney U test comparing CRISPR gene effect scores (deficient vs. proficient)
- Cohen's d effect size with 95% CI
- Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction across all tests
- Positive controls: PARP1, RAD51C, RAD51D, XRCC2, XRCC3, POLQ, RPA1, POLB
- Negative control: 53BP1/SHLD loss should reduce PARP1 dependency in BRCA-deficient lines

### Phase 3: Genome-Wide Non-HR Dependency Screen

~18,000 genes screened per cancer type and BRCA group:
- Same statistical framework as Phase 2
- FDR < 0.05 threshold yielded no hits (power limitation); nominal threshold (p < 0.05, |d| > 0.3) used
- Priority targets: POLB (BER), cGAS-STING pathway (CGAS, STING1, TBK1, IRF3)
- SHLD stratification: Top 30 hits tested for retained efficacy in SHLD-lost (PARPi-resistant) context
- Pathway categorization: DNA repair (non-HR), metabolic, epigenetic, cell cycle, immune/signaling
- BRCA1-specific vs. BRCA2-specific vs. shared hit classification

### Phase 4: PRISM Drug Sensitivity

- Named PARPi and DDR agents queried in PRISM 24Q2
- Genome-wide drug screen (same Mann-Whitney/Cohen's d framework)
- SHLD and pre-RC covariates for drug response heterogeneity
- Gene-drug concordance analysis

### Phase 5: TCGA Clinical Integration

- Pan-cancer BRCA1/2 mutation frequencies from TCGA
- US patient population estimates (TCGA frequency x SEER incidence)
- Co-mutation landscape (TP53, PTEN, PIK3CA, KRAS, and cancer-specific drivers)
- Cross-atlas references to TP53, PTEN, and WRN-MSI atlases

## Discussion

This atlas provides the first systematic genome-wide map of synthetic lethal dependencies in BRCA1/2-deficient cell lines across all cancer types in DepMap 25Q3. While the core hypothesis — that non-HR dependencies exist beyond the well-characterized PARP inhibitor axis — is supported by the identification of 3,709 novel candidate genes, the results are fundamentally limited by statistical power.

### Positive Control Validation

The failure of all 8 positive controls to reach FDR significance is a critical finding. PARP1 (d = -1.12), XRCC2 (d = -1.21), and POLQ (d = -1.03) showed effect sizes consistent with synthetic lethality, but the small number of BRCA-deficient lines per cancer type (6-13) could not overcome multiple testing correction. This does not invalidate the BRCA-PARP1 SL relationship — which is clinically proven — but it demonstrates that DepMap's cell line inventory is insufficient for powered per-lineage BRCA SL analyses at FDR-controlled thresholds. Future studies should consider pan-cancer meta-analysis approaches that pool evidence across lineages.

### BRCA1 vs. BRCA2 Differential Biology

The distinct SL profiles between BRCA1 (1,252 unique genes) and BRCA2 (1,687 unique genes) with only 200 shared is one of the most striking findings. The enrichment of FA pathway genes (FANCF, FANCL, FANCM) among BRCA1-specific hits is biologically coherent: BRCA1 participates in the FA pathway through FANCD2 monoubiquitination and end resection, creating a synthetic dependency when BRCA1 is lost. BRCA2's role is more downstream (RAD51 loading), explaining the divergent hit profiles. This has clinical implications — BRCA1-mutant and BRCA2-mutant patients may respond differently to non-PARPi targeted therapies.

### cGAS-STING Pathway

STING1 (d = -0.99) and TBK1 (d = -1.09) in BRCA2-deficient bowel cancer represent intriguing candidates. BRCA-deficient cells accumulate cytoplasmic DNA through replication stress, activating cGAS-STING signaling. In a tumor context this drives CD8+ T cell recruitment, but in cell lines (without immune cells) the signal reflects cell-autonomous growth effects. The large effect sizes despite non-significance suggest these genes merit follow-up in isogenic CRISPR validation models. The specificity to BRCA2 (not BRCA1) and Bowel (not other lineages) requires further investigation. Caution is warranted given the retraction of a key POLtheta inhibitor + immune checkpoint combination paper (Nature Communications 2023).

### PARPi-Resistant Context

The identification of 10/30 top SL genes retaining efficacy in SHLD-lost (PARPi-resistant) BRCA-deficient lines is clinically relevant. FA pathway genes (FANCF, FANCM, FAAP24) and XRCC1 retained dependency in this resistant context, suggesting they target mechanisms orthogonal to the HR restoration that drives PARPi resistance. CDIN1 showed the strongest signal in SHLD-lost lines (d = -0.88, p = 0.009), warranting further investigation as a target for PARPi-refractory patients.

### Drug Sensitivity and Clinical Translation

The PRISM analysis was severely limited by the absence of PARPi compounds, preventing direct validation of the core BRCA-PARP SL axis in drug space. The modest ATR inhibitor signal and the identification of afuresertib (AKT inhibitor, d = -0.53) as a BRCA-selective compound are consistent with the clinical rationale for PI3K/AKT + PARPi combinations currently in trials. The emergence of POLtheta inhibitors (ART6043, FDA Fast Track February 2026; novobiocin Phase 1) provides new clinical avenues for BRCA-mutant patients, and the positive POLQ effect size (d = -1.03) in this atlas supports the mechanistic basis.

### Clinical Opportunity

The estimated 45,223 BRCA-mutant US patients/year across 15 cancer types, with 13,505 in cancer types lacking approved PARPi, defines substantial unmet clinical need. Colorectal (3,056/year), bladder (2,079/year), and endometrial (2,036/year) are the largest unaddressed populations. The non-HR dependencies identified here — particularly FA pathway genes and cGAS-STING components — offer starting points for therapeutic development in these populations.

## Limitations

1. **Statistical power:** The primary limitation. With 6-18 BRCA-deficient lines per cancer type, no results survived FDR correction. All genome-wide hits use nominal p-values and should be treated as hypothesis-generating.
2. **Binary BRCA classification:** Epigenetic silencing (BRCA1 promoter methylation), LOH, and somatic reversions were not captured, potentially misclassifying some lines.
3. **No independent validation:** The project auto-advanced through analysis and validation without separate review. Results have not been validated in isogenic models, orthogonal screens, or patient-derived samples.
4. **PRISM PARPi gap:** The absence of PARPi in PRISM 24Q2 prevented direct validation of the core synthetic lethality axis in drug sensitivity space.
5. **Cell line limitations:** DepMap cell lines may not represent the full diversity of BRCA-mutant tumors, particularly rare subtypes. In vitro screens cannot capture immune-mediated effects (relevant for cGAS-STING findings).
6. **SHLD stratification underpowered:** Only 18 SHLD-lost lines among 99 BRCA-deficient, further split across cancer types, makes SHLD-stratified analyses exploratory.
7. **Drug identity unknown:** The 20 combination candidate compounds are identified by BRD IDs without annotation of their molecular targets or mechanisms.

## Next Steps

1. **Pan-cancer meta-analysis:** Pool BRCA SL signals across cancer types using fixed/random-effects models to overcome per-lineage power limitations.
2. **Isogenic validation:** Test top FA pathway hits (FANCE, FANCF, FANCL) and cGAS-STING targets (STING1, TBK1) in isogenic BRCA1/BRCA2-knockout cell line pairs.
3. **PARPi combination screens:** Validate POLB as a PARPi sensitizer and test whether CDIN1, FANCF, or XRCC1 knockdown synergizes with olaparib in BRCA-deficient models.
4. **SHLD-loss resistance models:** Characterize which SL genes retain efficacy in BRCA-deficient/53BP1-loss double-mutant models — these are the most clinically needed targets.
5. **Drug target annotation:** Map the 20 BRCA-selective combination candidate compounds (by BRD ID) to molecular targets using ChEMBL/PubChem to enable mechanistic interpretation.
6. **Expanded BRCA classification:** Incorporate BRCA1 promoter methylation, expression-based HRD scores, and somatic reversion detection for more accurate classification.
7. **Clinical trial alignment:** Cross-reference findings with ongoing PARPi combination trials (ATRi + PARPi in CAPRI; POLthetai + PARPi in ART6043 Phase 2) to identify biomarker hypotheses.
